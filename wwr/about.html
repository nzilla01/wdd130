White-water rafting originated in the 19th century when explorers used rafts for river navigation. The modern recreational activity emerged in the mid-20th century in the United States, with commercial rafting trips becoming popular. The trend spread globally, leading to the establishment of rafting industries in various countries. Safety measures and regulations were later implemented to ensure participant well-being. Today, white-water rafting is a popular adventure sport enjoyed worldwide.
